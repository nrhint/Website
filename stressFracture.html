Stress Fractures:

Stress fractures are fatigue-induced fracture of the bone caused by repeted stress and trauma over time. This can happen when repating submaximal loading such as running and jumping.

Symptoms:

Stress fractures are usauly found after a sudden increase in activity. They most comminly are manifest in weight bearing joints. Pain usauly subsides with rest but may be constantly present with a more severe injury. There is usauly an area of localized tenderness around the joint and tendon and swelling in the area.

Information about Stress fractures

Bones are constantly reforming as they break down and improving to the activities that you particiate in. This causes stress in different parts
